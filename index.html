What I found

A few things I noticed
while exploring the product.

Before writing anything, I spent time going through the Activities and Amusements section of District across Delhi NCR, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and Jaipur, paying attention to where the experience felt incomplete from a user's point of view.

01
Activity listings have no reviews or ratings
When someone is deciding whether to visit an amusement park or book a go-kart session, they naturally want to hear from people who have been there. Right now that information is not available inside District, which means users tend to go elsewhere to find it before coming back to book.
02
There is no way to plan an outing as a group
Most amusement and activity visits happen with friends or family, not alone. All the coordination currently happens outside the app, usually over WhatsApp, and quite a few plans end up not happening at all because the process is a bit chaotic.
03
District Play could benefit from a subscription layer
Products like FitPass have shown that a monthly slot-booking pass works really well for active users. Something similar for sports courts, run clubs, and fitness experiences does not seem to exist yet in India, and District Play feels well placed to explore it.

Opportunity Brief
🏃

District Play Pass: A Note for the Play Team

While exploring the Activities vertical, I noticed something interesting about District Play and put together a short opportunity brief for the Play team. It explores what an active lifestyle subscription could look like if it covered gyms, sports courts, run clubs, cycling groups, and yoga studios all on one pass. Run clubs in particular seem to be a fast-growing format with no subscription product covering them yet anywhere in India. Intended for the District Play team specifically.
